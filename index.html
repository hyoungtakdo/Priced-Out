Priced Out: Washington’s Housing Affordability Crisis

Washington’s housing market has changed dramatically since the early 2010s. Using Zillow county level housing data, we can see the massive and dramtic changes and rise in the cost of buying a home, which counties are the most expensive today, and how much more expensive homes are now compared to the early 2010s.

1. Home values climbed sharply in Washington’s most expensive counties

Down below you will see the average cost of homes in each county from the early 2010s to the present days. As you can see, the dramtic rise in price for homes did not happen instantly. Instead, prices rose over time and accelerated in recent years, making homeownership harder for many residents and for some, impossible.

2. Washington’s most expensive counties are not evenly distributed

Here, we take a look closer at the top ten counties with the highest current Zillow home values. The visualization helps show that housing pressure is especially concentrated in certain counties, often near major job centers and high demand regions.

3. Homes are not just expensive, they are far more expensive than they used to be

This chart compares home values in 2010 with the most recent values for the highest priced Washington counties. A simple before and after view of long term housing change to get us thinking on how much harder it got for the general public to afford a home of their own.

Why this matters

Housing is not only a private financial issue. It affects where people can live, how long they commute, whether families can build wealth, and whether communities remain accessible to workers, students, and longtime residents. Public housing data can help make these changes visible and support more informed conversations about affordability in Washington.

Data source and credit

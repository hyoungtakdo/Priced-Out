## Overview

Housing affordability has become one of the most pressing challenges facing Washington residents. Over the past two decades, home values have increased dramatically across many counties, making homeownership increasingly difficult for first time buyers, young families, and lower income households.

This project uses Zillow Home Value Index (ZHVI) data to explore how housing prices have changed throughout Washington State. Through a series of interactive visualizations, readers can examine long term housing trends, compare the state's most expensive counties, and understand how much home values have increased since 2010.


## Research Question

**How has housing affordability changed across Washington State over time, and which counties have experienced the most dramatic increases in home values since 2010?**


## Key Findings

### 1. Home Values Have Risen Dramatically Across Washington State

The line chart shows that home values have steadily increased across Washington's most expensive counties. While growth occurred gradually during the early 2000s, prices accelerated significantly during the last decade.

### 2. Housing Costs Are Concentrated in a Small Number of Counties

The bar chart highlights the counties with the highest current Zillow Home Value Index values. Many of the most expensive counties are located near major employment centers and areas experiencing strong population growth.

### 3. Homes Today Are Substantially More Expensive Than in 2010

The comparison chart illustrates the scale of housing inflation by comparing 2010 home values with present day values. In many counties, home values have more than doubled, demonstrating the growing affordability challenges facing Washington residents.


## Why This Matters

Housing affordability affects far more than homeownership. Rising housing costs influence where people live, how far they commute, their ability to build wealth, and whether communities remain accessible to long time residents.

As housing costs continue to increase throughout Washington, understanding these trends is important for residents, policymakers, planners, and community organizations seeking solutions to affordability challenges.

This project uses publicly available data to help make these trends visible and accessible to a broader audience.
